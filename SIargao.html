Where the Ocean Meets the Soul: A Siargao Escape


I made my way to Siargao. I wanted to get away from the hustle of deadlines and the bright city lights, so I was running away from the silence. Right then and there, from the moment I stepped off the plane, the island promised me swaying coconut trees, warm smiles from locals, and the rippling sweet hum of motorbikes winding down the coast. I woke up barefoot to surf the famous Cloud 9 boardwalk where surfers were surfing and the sunrise turned the morning hours with soft tones of orange light! I tried surfing at Jacking Horse, fell gloriously, laughed with strangers, and felt I was finally back to where I had been, what I had lost for so long: Freedom.

Siargao was more than just surfing to me, it was a wonder I was supposed to unravel. We hopped from island to island—Naked, Guyam, Daku—each one more magical than the last. At Magpupungko Rock Pools, I dove into crystal-clear water surrounded by cliffs, and at the Maasin River, I swung from a coconut tree into the calm, green current below. There were no clocks, no rushing, just those moments that felt long and slow were interspersed with copious amounts of sea breeze, mountain shakes (Aka milkshake) and conversation that just felt wrapped around me like a fuzzy blanket that I could have swayed to forever.

On my last night, I did a beach clean-up with some locals and other travelers. It seemed like the right thing to do, to give back to a place that gave me so much. Siargao wasn’t just a place to visit: it was a soft reminder of what simplicity and beauty in life can look like. As the waves lapped against the shore, I thought about how I didn’t just visit Siargao, I became a part of Siargao. And in many ways, I think Siargao became a part of me too.
