Long Trip, Big Reward: My Boracay Getaway


Landing on Boracay’s coast, with its powdery sands, took my breath away. I knew I was in a special place. Nestled in the province of Aklan in the Western Visayas region of the Philippines, this small island is famous for its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters. And yes! It's a long trip for me, the plane ride and van transfers but the moment I stepped on the island, it was all worth it.

The sun was beginning to set, coloring the clear blue sea golden, and all was quiet. I stayed in Station 1, where the beach was perfect to relax after the thrill of travel. I was in Boracay for the first time, and already falling in love with the relaxed, tropical beat of island life.

Each day started with the sound of gentle waves outside my window and ended with breathtaking sunsets. I spent hours just walking along the shore, drinking fresh mango shakes, and trying local snacks like the famous chori burger. One afternoon, I joined an island-hopping tour that includes cliff diving, snorkeling, and exploring hidden beaches I never knew existed.

The highlight for me was the paraw sailing during sunset, where the orange sky reflected off the water, making everything feel like a dream. It was in those quiet moments that I felt completely at peace. Boracay taught me how to slow down and appreciate the simple joys, sunshine, laughter, and time for myself. On my last night, I sat on the beach and watched the stars, grateful for the escape. Leaving wasn’t easy, but I knew I’d carry the memories with me forever. If you ever need a break from the noise of everyday life, Boracay is a place that will welcome you with open arms and sandy toes.
