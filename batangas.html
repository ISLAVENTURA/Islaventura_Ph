Chasing Sunsets: A Weekend in Calatagan, Batangas


This April, we packed our bags, hit the road early, and set off for a much-needed escape to Calatagan, Batangas. The trip wasn’t just about the destination, it started with a quick stop at one of the most iconic road trip landmarks in the South: the Kaybiang Tunnel. One minute, you’re squinting at zigzagging coastal roads, palms whipping past your window. The next, complete darkness. Surrounded by lush greenery and echoing with the sounds of passing cars and laughter, it gave the drive a cinematic feel. We couldn’t resist pulling over for a photo and soaking in the cool tunnel air before continuing on.

The journey to Calatagan was smooth and scenic. No rows of resorts, no jet skis roaring, rolling hills, quiet towns, glimpses of the sea and curve of bone-white sand made the ride surprisingly peaceful. When we finally arrived, the warm coastal breeze welcomed us like an old friend.

Calatagan offered everything we needed: clear skies, calm waters, and time to slow down. We spent most of our time by the beach—walking along the shore, taking in the golden hour, and letting the waves wash away the week’s stress. The sunsets there weren’t just beautiful. They were grounding. When night time came, Ate Lita’s Grill Spot materialized down the beach. No sign, just smoke and the smell of garlic rice hitting the air. We ate with sandy fingers: grilled liempo sticky with sweetness, squid so tender it melted, and buko juice straight from the nut. I was so full that I could barely walk back to the inn that we're staying in.

What made the trip even better was its simplicity. No complicated itinerary, no rush. Just good company, good food, and views that reminded us why local travel is worth it.

Would I go back? Absolutely. And next time, I might stay a little longer.
